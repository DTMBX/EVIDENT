In Honor of Religious Martyrs & Pioneers

St. Thomas Becket (1118-1170)
Murdered in Canterbury Cathedral for defending the Church against state tyranny. His martyrdom established the principle that conscience stands above earthly power‚Äîa truth that would echo through centuries and cross an ocean to shape American liberty.

Pilgrims of Plymouth (1620)
102 souls fled England's religious persecution, crossing the Atlantic to find freedom of worship on the real land of North America. Half died the first winter. Their sacrifice planted the seed of religious liberty in American soil.

Roger Williams (1603-1683)
Banished from Massachusetts Bay Colony for demanding separation of church and state. Founded Rhode Island as a haven for "liberty of conscience." The First Amendment's religious freedom clause owes its existence to his exile.

Quakers, Catholics, Jews, and Religious Minorities
Whipped, imprisoned, hanged, and exiled in the American colonies for their faith. Their persecution taught the Founders that religious freedom must be absolute and protected by law‚Äînot subject to majority opinion.

Military Chaplains (1775-Present)
Clergy who served in uniform, ministering to soldiers of all faiths and none. From Revolutionary War to present day, over 400 military chaplains have died in service, defending the right of every American to worship‚Äîor not‚Äîaccording to conscience.
